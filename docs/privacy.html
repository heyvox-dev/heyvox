Privacy Policy

HeyVox and the Hush Chrome extension · Last updated: 20 April 2026

This policy covers both HeyVox, the desktop voice layer for AI coding agents, and Hush, the companion Chrome extension that pauses in-browser media while HeyVox is speaking or listening. The short version: nothing you do with either product is sent anywhere, ever.

Summary — the one-paragraph version

HeyVox and Hush are 100% local. We do not collect, store, transmit, sell, or share any personal data. Your microphone audio, speech transcriptions, browsing activity, tab contents, configuration, and usage patterns never leave your computer. There are no servers operated by us that process any of your data. There are no analytics, no telemetry, no ads, and no user accounts.

Data we collect

None. There is no account creation, no signup, no login, no usage reporting. HeyVox runs entirely on your Mac; Hush runs entirely inside your copy of Chrome and a local companion process on your Mac.

Audio, speech, and transcription

  • Wake-word detection runs locally on-device via openwakeword.
  • Speech-to-text runs locally on-device via MLX Whisper (Apple Silicon) or sherpa-onnx (Intel).
  • Text-to-speech runs locally on-device via Kokoro or Piper.
  • No audio, no transcripts, no model outputs, and no training data are transmitted off your device. Nothing is shared with Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, Apple, or anyone else.
  • A local transcript history (if enabled) is stored in ~/.config/heyvox/ on your Mac. Deleting that file or the config directory removes it.

Browser data (Hush extension)

Hush reads the tab.audible property of tabs to find which one is producing audio, and executes a small pause/resume script against the matching tab. Hush does not:

  • Read tab content, page HTML, form contents, passwords, cookies, or any other page data.
  • Read or store URLs, titles, or browsing history.
  • Send any data over the network.

The <all_urls> host permission is used only because media can play on any site. The extension does not examine or transmit anything about the sites you visit.

Native messaging

Hush talks to its desktop companion via Chrome's standard nativeMessaging API. This is a process-to-process channel on your local machine — it does not reach the internet. Messages contain only pause/resume commands and acknowledgements; no user content is exchanged.

Model downloads

First-time setup downloads speech-recognition and text-to-speech models from their respective open-source repositories (Hugging Face, GitHub). These are one-off downloads directly from those hosts — we do not proxy them, log them, or see that they happened. You can audit the download URLs in the source code at github.com/heyvox-dev/heyvox.

Analytics, crash reporting, telemetry

None. There is no analytics integration, no crash reporter phoning home, no feature-usage tracking, no error beaconing. If something crashes, the stack trace stays on your Mac.

Third parties

We do not share any data with third parties, because we do not collect any. The open-source speech and text-to-speech models (Whisper, Kokoro, Piper, openwakeword, sherpa-onnx) run entirely on your device and are licensed under their respective open-source licences.

Children's privacy

HeyVox and Hush are developer tools not directed at children under 13. Because we do not collect any personal data, we do not knowingly collect data from children or anyone else.

International users / GDPR / CCPA

HeyVox and Hush do not collect personal data, so there is no personal data to subject to cross-border transfer, access, or deletion requests. You are welcome to delete the applications, the Chrome extension, and the ~/.config/heyvox directory at any time and no data will remain on any system we control (because no data is on any system we control).

Changes to this policy

If we ever materially change how HeyVox or Hush handles data, we will update this page and the "Last updated" date at the top. Since the software is open-source, any such change would also be visible in the public repository's commit history at github.com/heyvox-dev/heyvox.

Contact

Questions, concerns, or anything else: email hello@heyvox.dev or open an issue at github.com/heyvox-dev/heyvox/issues.
